<p><p>Automotive window regulators come in two main types: manual and automated. Manual regulators require the driver or passenger to physically turn a crank or push a lever to raise or lower the window. In contrast, automated regulators use an electric motor to easily operate the window with the push of a button. Both types have their advantages and disadvantages, with manual regulators being simpler and more cost-effective, while automated regulators offer greater convenience and ease of use. The choice between the two depends on the preferences and needs of the consumer.</p></p>
