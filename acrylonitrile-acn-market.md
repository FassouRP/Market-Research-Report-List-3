<p><p>Acrylonitrile (ACN) is primarily produced using two methods: the propylene method and the propane method. The propylene method involves the reaction of propylene with ammonia and air over a catalyst, while the propane method involves the oxidation of propane with ammonia and air. Both methods produce high-quality acrylonitrile with slight variations in production costs and energy consumption. The choice between the two methods depends on factors such as availability and cost of feedstocks, market demand, and environmental regulations.</p></p>
