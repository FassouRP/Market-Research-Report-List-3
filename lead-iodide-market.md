<p><p>Lead iodide is an inorganic chemical compound with the formula PbI2. It is a yellow, crystalline solid that is sparingly soluble in water. It is also used in the manufacturing of X-ray and gamma-ray detectors, as well as in organic light-emitting diodes (OLEDs) and optoelectronic devices.</p><p>The global lead iodide market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for clean and renewable energy sources is driving the growth of the solar power industry, thereby boosting the demand for lead iodide in solar cells. Additionally, the rising adoption of OLED displays in various consumer electronics, such as smartphones and televisions, is also fueling the market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, the growing healthcare industry and the increasing use of X-ray and gamma-ray detectors for diagnostic imaging are contributing to the demand for lead iodide. The compound's excellent radiation detection properties make it ideal for use in these applications.</p><p>The market is witnessing several trends that are expected to shape its growth trajectory.
