<p><p>Architectural acoustic panels are used to improve sound quality by reducing noise levels and controlling reverberation within a space. There are three main types of acoustic panels on the market: Acoustic Membranes, Resonators Panel, and Porous Material Panel. Acoustic membranes are thin panels that vibrate to absorb sound waves. Resonator panels use enclosed air cavities to trap and dissipate sound energy. Porous material panels utilize materials like foam or fabric to absorb sound waves and reduce noise levels. Each type of panel offers different benefits for sound control in architectural spaces.</p></p>
