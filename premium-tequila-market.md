<p><p>The premium tequila market is typically categorized into four main types: Blanco, Reposado, Joven, and Anejo. Blanco is unaged and known for its purity and crisp taste. Reposado is aged in oak barrels for a few months, giving it a smoother and more complex flavor. Joven, also known as gold tequila, is a blend of Blanco and Reposado. Anejo is aged for at least one year, resulting in a rich and oaky flavor. Other types in the market may include Extra Anejo, Cristalino, or artisanal small-batch tequilas.</p></p>
