<p><p>Soluble meal fibers are a diverse group of market types that offer various health benefits. Insulin, a type of soluble fiber, aids in controlling blood sugar levels and can be found in foods like beans and whole grains. Polydextrose, another soluble fiber, can improve bowel function and is often used as a sugar replacement in low-calorie products. Pectin, commonly present in fruits, acts as a natural thickening agent and can help with cholesterol management. Other market types include gums, such as guar gum and gum arabic, which can enhance the texture and stability of food products. These soluble meal fibers contribute to a healthy diet by providing added nutrients and improving digestive health.</p></p>
