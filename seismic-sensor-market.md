<p><p>Seismic sensors are devices used to detect and measure seismic waves caused by earthquakes or other sources of ground motion. Inertial seismometers are a type of seismic sensor that measure ground motion by detecting changes in acceleration. Extensometers, on the other hand, measure ground deformation by detecting changes in distance between fixed points. Both types of sensors play a crucial role in monitoring seismic activity and helping to mitigate the impact of earthquakes and other natural disasters.</p></p>
