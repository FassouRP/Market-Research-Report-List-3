<p><strong>The Vascular Access Device Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Central Vascular Access Devices</li><li>Peripheral Vascular Access Devices</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Vascular Access Device market encompasses two main types: Central Vascular Access Devices and Peripheral Vascular Access Devices. Central Vascular Access Devices are used to administer medications, fluids, and nutrients directly into the large central veins near the heart. They are suitable for long-term treatments and can be placed in various locations, including the chest, neck, or groin. Peripheral Vascular Access Devices, on the other hand, are inserted into the peripheral veins, commonly found in the arms and hands, for short-term treatments such as blood transfusions or administering antibiotics. Both types serve important purposes in delivering medical interventions.</p></p>
