<p><p>Brake friction discs are classified into three main types based on the material they are made of: metallic discs, ceramic discs, and composite discs. Metallic discs are durable and provide good performance, but can cause more wear on other brake components. Ceramic discs offer excellent heat resistance and performance, making them suitable for high-performance vehicles. Composite discs combine the benefits of both metallic and ceramic discs, offering a balance between durability, performance, and heat resistance. Each type of disc caters to different needs and preferences in the automotive market.</p></p>
