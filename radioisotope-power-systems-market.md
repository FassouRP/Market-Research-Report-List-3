<p><p>Radioisotope Power Systems (RPS) utilize the decay of radioisotopes to generate electrical power, making them suitable for remote or space missions. The RPS market primarily consists of three types: -238Pu, 90Sr, and other isotopes. -238Pu employs plutonium-238 as a fuel source, offering high energy density and long half-life. 90Sr, using strontium-90, offers lower energy density but a shorter half-life. Other isotopes include americium-241 and polonium-210. Each isotope has different characteristics, providing varying power outputs and durations. The choice of isotope depends on specific mission requirements and safety considerations.</p></p>
