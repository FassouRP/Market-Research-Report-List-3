<p><p>Systemic scleroderma is a rare autoimmune disease characterized by the hardening and tightening of the skin and connective tissues. The treatment market for this condition includes various approaches. Immunosuppressors are medications that suppress the immune system to reduce inflammation. Phosphodiesterase 5 inhibitors (PHA) improve blood flow and reduce symptoms. Endothelin receptor antagonists and prostacyclin analogues help relax blood vessels, improving blood circulation. Calcium channel blockers are used to manage Raynaud's phenomenon, a common symptom of scleroderma. Other treatments may include physical therapy, pain management, and organ-specific therapies. These treatment options aim to alleviate symptoms and slow disease progression.</p></p>
