<p><p>Tinning lines are used in the manufacturing industry to apply a thin layer of tin onto metal surfaces for corrosion resistance and solderability. There are two main types of tinning lines: hot-dip tinning lines and electrolytic tinning lines. Hot-dip tinning lines involve immersing the metal in a bath of molten tin, while electrolytic tinning lines use an electrolytic process to deposit tin onto the metal surface. Both types of tinning lines are essential in producing high-quality metal products for various industries.</p></p>
