<p><p>Inverters can be categorized into different market types based on their voltage capacity and application. Middle and Low Voltage Frequency Converters are designed to convert electrical power from a lower voltage level (typically up to 1 kV) to a higher frequency. These inverters are commonly used in various industrial applications, such as motor drives, HVAC systems, and renewable energy generation. On the other hand, High Voltage Frequency Converters are capable of converting power at higher voltage levels (typically above 1 kV) to a different frequency, and are typically used in large-scale industrial power systems or grid applications.</p></p>
